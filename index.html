Step 4 of 5
Serum Thyroglobulin & Antibodies
Post-operative Biochemistry (6–12 weeks after surgery)
Reference: Assay sensitivity should be ≤0.1 ng/mL. If TgAb positive, Tg is unreliable (may be falsely low).
After lobectomy: routine Tg measurement is not recommended as a tumour marker (Rec 9, 47D) — the intact remnant lobe secretes Tg, so a single value cannot distinguish normal lobe from disease. If measured, only the trend over time (rising on a stable assay) is informative. You may leave this blank; neck ultrasound is the primary surveillance tool here.
Interpretation note: Rising TgAb is a surrogate for biochemically incomplete response even in the absence of localizable disease. Stable/declining TgAb may represent indeterminate response. Positive TgAb makes Tg measurement unreliable.

Recurrent / Persistent Disease
Recurrence Management Pathway
This pathway applies the ATA 2025 management algorithm for structurally identified recurrent or persistent disease (Recs 53–61). Acute management is driven mainly by the recurrent lesion's characteristics. Original histology, if known, refines RAI-avidity expectations and targeted-therapy choice — but it is optional: the tool answers fully without it.
Original Tumour Histology — optional, if known
Aggressive subtypes (tall cell, columnar, OTC, poorly differentiated) predict lower RAI avidity and stronger FDG-PET utility (Rec 50A). Leave blank if this is a first visit and the original report is not on hand — management will proceed regardless.
